01 — Problem

The Challenge

Retail health advisors can't scale. Shoppers need consistent, 24/7 guidance — static posters can't deliver it.

⚠️

No Consistent Guidance

Shoppers asking about Sensodyne, Panadol, or Voltaren get inconsistent advice depending on who's available — or no advice at all.

🕐

No 24/7 Availability

Human pharmacists aren't always present in large retail environments. The gap is costly and the need is constant around the clock.

🌳

Complex Symptom Trees

Oral care, cold & flu, and migraine each need multi-step diagnostic flows — too complex for a FAQ, too nuanced for a poster.

Digital Avatar solves all three — a fully interactive, voice-activated digital health avatar that scales infinitely, never sleeps, and guides every shopper to the right Haleon product.
02 — Solution

Meet Digital Avatar

A real-time Unity3D avatar powered by voice and intent matching — six core systems working as one.

— 01

Voice Input

Listens via Windows Speech API with 4 mic modes: Off, Always-On, Push-to-Talk, and Voice Activation with adaptive noise thresholds.

WindowsSpeechToText.cs
— 02

Intent Matching

Keyword scoring detects Oral Care / Cold & Flu / Migraine from natural speech with tie-breaking logic for ambiguous inputs.

IntentMatcher.cs
— 03

Guided Flows

Multi-step yes/no question trees guide users through up to 10 Q&A steps per health domain to the right Haleon product.

NPCResponseController.cs
— 04

Live Subtitles

Animated typed subtitles sync to audio with fade transitions. Letter mode or word mode, regex-split per sentence.

SubtitleUI.cs
— 05

Product Popups

Contextual product images (Sensodyne, Panadol, Voltaren) appear at the right moment via PopupImageUI with 4s fade cycle.

PopupImageUI.cs
— 06

Camera System

Cinemachine multi-angle cameras switch for cinematic presentation. Single button cycles through all virtual camera angles.

CinemachineCameraSwitcher.cs
🤖

Haleon Health Assistant

11 — Team Activity

Q&A + Team Quiz

Common questions about the project — and a live quiz to test your team's understanding.

What makes Digital Avatar different from a chatbot?
+
A Digital Avatar is a real-time Unity3D avatar — she speaks, listens, and animates. She runs entirely locally on a Windows kiosk, uses Cinemachine cameras for cinematic angles, and has a full 3D presence with subtitles, product popups, and voice-driven flows. No cloud dependency, no lag.
Does it require internet to work?
+
No. Digital Avatar uses Windows Speech API which processes voice locally. Intent matching runs in C# locally. All conversation flows are hardcoded in NPCResponseData. The system is fully offline — ideal for secure retail kiosk environments.
How does she know what health issue the user has?
+
IntentMatcher.cs scores keyword hits per health domain (Oral Care, Cold & Flu, Migraine). Each domain has a weighted keyword list. The highest-scoring domain wins, with tie-breaking logic to handle ambiguous inputs like "I have pain".
How many people does Digital Avatar work with at once?
+
One person at a time per kiosk — designed for 1-on-1 guided conversations. You can deploy multiple kiosks in parallel, each running its own independent instance, scaling to any retail footprint.
Can we add new products or health topics?
+
Yes. New domains: (1) add IntentType enum value, (2) add keyword arrays to IntentMatcher, (3) define Q&A flow in NPCResponseData. Product images swap in PopupImageUI. No engine rebuild needed.
What's the camera system for?
+
CinemachineCameraSwitcher cycles through Cinemachine virtual cameras. One button cuts between cinematic angles — close-up, wide shot, side profile — making the demo feel broadcast-quality rather than a static screen.
